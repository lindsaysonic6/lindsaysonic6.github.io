Yep, I Was Once an Engineer


Or an engineering major to be exact. For the first two years of my college experience, I was a Biomedical Engineering (BME) major at Georgia Tech. While I admire the craft, engineering just was not for me. During our projects, I always found myself drifting towards the design and writing portions over the math and hard science portions. I love the creativity of brainstorming and sketching solutions, so eventually I came to the decision to follow what I love and packed up bags and moved over to Computational Media. However, I still learned a plethora of valuable skills during my time in BMED.


Here’s a quick rundown:


BMED 2310: Redesigning Nasal Cannulas to Improve Patient Experience

  • Proficient understanding of product design including researching a physiological problem and competitor devices on the market, interviewing users to find and prioritize their needs, modelling the device through SolidWorks, and building and testing prototypes under FDA regulations

  • Basic comprehension of the physiological impact of Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease on the respiratory system and the mechanics of the oxygen therapy used to treat it

BMED 2250: Repurposing a Flex Sensor to Characterize Knee Osteoarthritis Through Knee Flexion Angles

  • Proficient understanding of the process behind identifying key physiological needs, mathematically modelling tendons as springs, hardwiring a device, and preparing a testing protocol to measure the specificity, sensitivity, and p-values of related variables using ANOVA and MATLAB

  • Basic comprehension of knee kinematics and osteoarthritis’ effects on the pathology of the knee and overall gait

  • Ability to develop psychological safety and effective communication with team to increase team productivity
